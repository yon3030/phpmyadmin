perimeter

New

continuous line forming the boundary of a shape not including shared pixels, or the minimum bounding box, whichever is shortest. For example, the perimeter calculation for a rectangle is 2h+2w -4, where h is the height and w is the width and the corners are not counted twice. The perimeter of a circle is 2𝜋r. For components which wrap onto multiple lines as part of a sentence or block of text (such as links), the perimeter can be established based on how the component would appear on a single line.
